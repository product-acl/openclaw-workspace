## High-Value Keywords (ranked by estimated volume)
1. **drinking game** — Core search term with high volume, used by competitors like Picolo and Drink and Tell
2. **party game** — Broad category with consistent search volume across both drinking and couples segments
3. **truth or dare** — Highly searched term, especially among younger audiences and party scenarios
4. **couples game** — Strong niche term with dedicated audience looking for relationship-focused games
5. **relationship quiz** — Specific intent for couples wanting to test compatibility and connection
6. **multiplayer game** — Important for social gaming aspect that both segments value
7. **fun with friends** — Natural language search term used by real users
8. **ice breaker** — Key use case for both party and couples scenarios
9. **date night** — Specific use case for couples segment
10. **social game** — Emphasizes the multiplayer, interactive nature

## Keyword Gaps (competitors miss these)
1. **dual-mode game** — No competitor positions as both party AND couples game
2. **card mechanics** — Most apps are Q&A based, not card-based with actual game mechanics
3. **paywall alternative** — Opportunity to position against Picolo's subscription model
4. **mechanics not questions** — Emphasizing game mechanics over simple Q&A
5. **party to couples** — Transition use case from group settings to intimate settings
6. **no subscription** — Free alternative messaging
7. **actual gameplay** — Differentiating from simple truth/dare/question apps
8. **two games in one** — Unique value proposition
